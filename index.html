Hard limits. In SAR operations the pressure to fly can be intense — use this to support your decision.
Pilot authority: Under CAA regulations, the Remote Pilot in Command has final authority on whether a flight proceeds. No instruction from search management, police, or any other party overrides the pilot's safety decision.

Involved Persons Briefing

Deliver this briefing to make personnel "involved persons" under CAA regulations — permitting closer drone proximity.
Regulatory basis: Under UK CAA regulations (Article 16 of the Air Navigation Order and the UAS Regulation), an "involved person" is someone who has been briefed on the operation and accepted the associated risks. Once briefed and consenting, the standard 50 m separation distance from uninvolved persons no longer applies to them — enabling effective close-proximity SAR operations.

⚠ This briefing is mandatory before any close-proximity drone work

  • Deliver this briefing verbally, clearly, before the operation begins
  • All persons must confirm understanding and consent — no exceptions
  • If anyone declines or has concerns, treat them as uninvolved — maintain full separation
  • Log the number of persons briefed in the flight record
Briefing Script — Read Aloud
1
Introduction & Purpose
"My name is [your name] and I am the Remote Pilot in Command for this operation. We are conducting a drone-assisted search as part of a WarkSAR callout. I need to give you a brief safety briefing so that you can work safely in the area where the drone will be operating."
Delivered — persons are attentive
2
Describe the Aircraft & Operation
"We will be flying a drone — a small multi-rotor unmanned aircraft — in and around your location. It weighs under 25 kg and is operated under a CAA Operational Authorisation. The drone may fly at low altitude and could come within a short distance of you during the search."
Point to or show the aircraft if possible — familiarity reduces startle responses.
Delivered — aircraft shown or described
3
Hazard Awareness
"There are some important safety points. The propellers are sharp and fast-moving — never approach the drone while it is powered or the motors are running. Do not reach up towards the aircraft at any time. If the drone needs to land near you, stand still and wait for my instruction — do not move towards it."
Hazards explained — questions answered
4
Emergency & Abort Procedures
"If you hear me or the spotter shout 'ABORT' or 'LAND NOW', move away from your current position immediately and take cover if you can. Do not look up at the aircraft — move first, then take cover. I will always try to land away from people, but your safety is the priority."
Ensure the abort signal is clearly understood — confirm it back verbally if time allows.
Abort signal confirmed understood
5
Consent & Right to Decline
"By staying in the operational area, you are confirming that you have understood this briefing and that you consent to the drone operating in close proximity to you. You are not obliged to remain in the area. If you have any concerns, please tell me now — I will keep the drone away from you and we will find another way to manage the operation."
Allow a moment for questions. Anyone who declines must be kept outside the 50 m separation zone for the entire operation.
Consent confirmed — all persons agreed or relocated
6
Ongoing Awareness
"During the operation, the spotter will monitor the airspace and keep you informed. If the search area changes or the drone needs to come much closer to you, we will update you at that point. Please keep an eye out and flag anything unusual to the spotter immediately."
Ongoing comms arrangement confirmed
7
Log & Record
"I am now recording in the flight log that [number] persons have received and understood this safety briefing and have consented to the drone operating in close proximity. The time is [time]."
Enter the number of persons in the field above. Record this in your written flight log with time, location, and names where practicable.
Logged — number of persons and time recorded in flight log
