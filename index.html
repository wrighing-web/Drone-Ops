On-Site Survey

Complete before any flight. Cannot be delegated to a non-pilot.

⚠ Legacy Drone — Check Before Every Flight

  • No IP rating — DO NOT fly in rain, mist, heavy dew, or wet fog
  • Check motor housings and shell for moisture before powering on
  • Confirm batteries are dry — do not use batteries stored in damp conditions
  • No obstacle avoidance — pilot must rely entirely on visual and spotter

Drone Pre-Flight

Complete immediately before each flight. Repeat for every battery change / re-launch.

⚠ Legacy Drone — Additional Checks

  • Inspect full airframe for cracks, stress marks, or damage from previous flights
  • Confirm firmware is current — legacy platforms do not auto-update
  • Compass calibration may be needed more frequently — check app warnings

Pilot & Crew Readiness

Completed by the Remote Pilot in Command before assuming operational control.
Lowland Rescue standard: WarkSAR operates under a CAA Operational Authorisation. Pilots must hold an A2 CofC (Open Category) or GVC (Specific Category, required for most SAR operations), and be current on deconfliction procedures with NPAS, air ambulance, and RAF low flying cell.

Abort Criteria & Go / No-Go

Hard limits. In SAR operations the pressure to fly can be intense — use this to support your decision.
Pilot authority: Under CAA regulations, the Remote Pilot in Command has final authority on whether a flight proceeds. No instruction from search management, police, or any other party overrides the pilot's safety decision.

⚠ Legacy Drone — Additional No-Fly Conditions

  • Any precipitation — rain, drizzle, sleet, snow, heavy mist or fog
  • High humidity, heavy dew, or condensation on the airframe
  • Moisture or corrosion on battery contacts
