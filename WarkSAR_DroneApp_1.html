⚠ Legacy Drone — Check Before Every Flight
  • No water resistance — DO NOT fly in rain, mist, heavy dew or fog
  • Check motor housings for moisture or condensation before powering on
  • Confirm batteries are dry — do not use if stored in damp conditions
  • May lack obstacle avoidance — rely on visual and spotter at all times
Regulatory basis: CAA ANO 2016 & UK UAS Regulations. The Remote Pilot must conduct a dynamic risk assessment before launching. This checklist fulfils that requirement.

Physical Inspection
All propellers inspected — no chips, cracks or warping CRITICAL
Damaged props are a leading cause of in-flight failure. Replace if any damage found.
All propellers securely fitted and locked CRITICAL
Check each prop is clicked/locked. Loose props can disengage in flight.
Airframe inspected — no cracks, loose screws or damage
Pay attention to arm joints and landing gear. Legacy drones are more likely to show fatigue.
Camera / gimbal inspected — lock removed, lens clean and undamaged
Motor mounts and arms free of debris, mud or moisture
Any moisture in motor housing must be fully dried before flight — no IP rating.

Flight battery ≥80% charged and firmly seated CRITICAL
Battery must be clicked securely. A loose battery can disconnect mid-flight.
Battery health checked — no swelling, damage or error warnings
Do not use swollen or very cold battery. Warm to at least 10°C in cold conditions.
Spare batteries accounted for, charged and accessible
Remote controller battery sufficient for full operation
